All you need to know about 10th grade

Board:

The 10th board exam, often referred to as the Secondary School Certificate (SSC) exam in India, is a crucial milestone in a student's academic journey. It typically covers subjects like Mathematics, Science, Social Studies, and Languages. The exam is conducted by various education boards, such as CBSE, and state boards, and serves as a basis for students to progress to higher education.
The 12th board exam, also known as the Higher Secondary Certificate (HSC) exam in India, is another significant milestone in a student's academic life. It is typically conducted by education boards like CBSE, and state boards. The exam covers a wide range of subjects based on the chosen stream (Science, Commerce, Arts).

Syllabus and Notes:

The syllabus for the 10th board exam varies depending on the education board. However, it generally includes core subjects like Mathematics, Science (Physics, Chemistry, Biology), Social Studies (History, Geography, Civics, Economics), and Languages (English and a second language specific to the region or board). The syllabus is designed to provide students with a strong foundation in these subjects and prepare them for higher studies or vocational courses.

Here's a more detailed breakdown of the typical subjects and syllabus for the 10th board exam in Bihar, focusing on the CBSE and ICSE and State boards:
1. Mathematics: Algebra: Polynomials, Linear Equations, Quadratic Equations, Arithmetic Progressions. - Geometry: Triangles, Circles, Constructions, Areas, Volumes. - Trigonometry: Trigonometric Ratios, Trigonometric Identities. - Mensuration: Areas and Volumes of 2D and 3D figures. - Statistics and Probability: Mean, Median, Mode, Probability.
2. Science:
-Physics: Light, Electricity, Magnetism, Energy, Sound.
-Chemistry: Chemical Reactions, Acids, Bases, Salts, Metals, Non-Metals.
- Biology: Life Processes, Control and Coordination, Heredity and Evolution, Environment, Natural Resources.
3. Social Studies:
-History: Nationalism in India, The First World War, etc.
-Geography: Resources and Development, Water Resources, Agriculture, Minerals and Energy Resources, etc.
-Civics: Power Sharing, Federalism, Democracy and Diversity, Gender, Religion, and Caste, etc.
-Economics: Development, Sectors of the Indian Economy, Money and Credit, Globalization, etc.
4. Languages: English: Reading Comprehension, Writing Skills, Grammar, Literature (Poems, Stories, Plays). - Second Language (Varies by region and board): like Hindi and Sanskrit.
